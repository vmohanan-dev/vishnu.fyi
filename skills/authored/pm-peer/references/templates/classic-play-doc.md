# Product 1-pager template

HOW TO USE THIS TEMPLATE

Use this document as a guide to help you think through plays, features, new ideas, or products. In an ideal situation, this doc must be created in collaboration with a triad (PM, Eng, UX) or team members. This is also sometimes called a product brief, feature brief, product spec, pitch, or PRD. Not every section needs to be filled out, so pick what you think is best. Ultimately, this becomes a base for brainstorming problem space and solutions space with UX/Engineering and arriving at designs and technical plans.

## What problem are we solving?

Provide an overview of the problem space in clear and concise language.

## Why now?

Why are we solving this problem now? What are the market/company/strategy considerations for prioritizing this problem?

## Who is it for?

Information about potential user personas that your play is targeting should be captured here. Only have 1-2 primary personas - sorted by order. List secondary personas briefly.

## Customer feedback & use cases

Was there any past customer feedback to support this case? What are the major customer use cases?

## Solution strategy & Scope

The solution space: discuss requirements at a very high level. Collaborate with engineering/UX on technical design and UX deliverables here.

### Requirements

As a high-level statement, a requirement describes what a product, system, or service must do or meet in order to function properly. Write requirements from the perspective of the end-user or customer, i.e. as user stories, to emphasize the value and experience the user will gain.

| Requirement | Priority | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Format: As a [type of user], I want [an action or feature] so that [a benefit or reason]. | P0, P1, P2, etc | Provide any details, context, and specifications that are crucial to understanding the requirement |

### Technical design

Collaborate with the engineering team to define required artifacts or RFCs to support this feature/product.

### UX Designs/Journey

Collaborate with a UX partner to define the UX journey and deliverables. If this is an API, then an API contract is also an important aspect of UX.

## Omissions/out-of-scope

[Optional] Highlight functionality excluded from the project scope. Clearly defining out-of-scope requirements helps prevent scope creep and ensures the project remains focused on its primary objectives.

## Dependencies

[Optional] Discuss dependencies with other teams. What type of alignment do you need?

## Competitive landscape: how are others solving it?

[Optional] How are others solving it? It can be direct competition or products that solve similar or adjacent problems. What are the pros and cons?

## Risks & mitigations

[Optional] Outline major risks with your thoughts on how to mitigate them.

## Measuring success

Think about what success means and how you would measure it. If this requires instrumentation, add that in too.

## Pricing & Packaging

[Optional] Work with GTM partner & leadership to align on the right pricing & packaging, if needed.

## Additional Resources

List any additional resources you want your readers to look at.
